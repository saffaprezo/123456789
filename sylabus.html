Edexcel IGCSE (O Level) English Language

(a) Subject choice / option

4EBO English Language Specification B

(b) Subject / Paper Description

A three hour themed paper made of three sections:

Section A – Comprehension (two or Three passages on a related subject / topic but may be different in text Audience, Purpose and Narrative Technique).

Students read passages for understanding meanings and questions are asked on identification of ideas, contextual meanings of words and expressions, as well as comparisms of passages on features of texts and sub-texts.

Care must be shown in answering ‘own word’ questions not to copy directly from the passages.

Practising past papers is of immense help for this section. Practise spending 40 – 45 minutes in this section.

Section B – Directed writing (single compulsory writing task). This section is writing according to guide lines and requires using materials only from a passage or passages but writing for a different audience and purpose. Marks are gained for appropriate language and style to the audience of the writing task: a letter, an article, a speech, a dialogue, a leaflet, a diary entry etc.

Avoid:

  • Lifting portions of the passage(s) in your directed writing
  • Using personal experience or ideas from your readings not found in the passage(s).
  • Directed writing is a form of summary and no marks are rewarded for additional materials not found in the passage(s).
  • Do not spend more than one hour in this section

Section C – Essay (An optional section of three questions to answer one). Spend about an hour in this section.

  • Students are expected to write more lucidly on a topic using their imagination, experience or reading in this 400 word length essay.
  • Avoid using materials or basing your essay on the comprehension passage(s) though you may use a central idea from the passage(s).
  • Questions may be on : Narrative Description, Argumentative, Speech, article etc.
    • Similarities between Section B and C

Both essays are similarly assessed on : content of essay, organization (appropriate lay-out, paragraphing, paragraph linkages, etc), use of appropriate expression and style of writing task (use of appropriate detail, clarity and coherence in expressions) and mechanical Accuracy (use and variety of punctuation marks, spellings, verb tense, subject and verb agreement, capitalization etc).

Edexcel IGCSE (O Level) English Literature

  1. Subject choice / option

4ETO / A (English Literature Papers 1 nd 2)

  1. Subject / Paper Description

4ETO Option A is made of English Literature papers 1 and 2.

Paper 1 Drama and Prose set for 1hr 45 minutes

Section A : Drama – William Shakespeare’s Romeo and Juliet

Section B : Prose – John Steinbeck’s Of Mic and Men

You are to answer one question on one text in each section and advised to spend 50 minutes on each question.

PAPER 1

Drama – Shakespeare’s Romeo and Juliet

Study Tips

(i) Genre – Romeo and Juliet as a Shakespearean tragedy

(ii) Plot – Story line of the play – look at how the development of the plot reveals character, conveys themes or ideas and generate response from you as audience.

(iii) Characterisation : understanding the roles of central characters as well as minor characters in the play.

(iv) The Themes : main ideas running through the play : Love, Hate, Fate, Conflict/Violence etc.

Prose : Steinbecks Of Mice and Men

Study Tips

  1. Understanding the American Context in the 1930’s
  2. Understand and follow the plot how it reveals the adventure and aspirations of the two main characters, interaction with other characters etc.
  3. Characterisation – presentation of different characters
  4. Themes: friendship, Dream, Loneliness, Social Prejudice etc
  5. Language – Steinbeck’s use of informal / collognial English and your understanding of characters, what dialogues reveal about characters etc.

PAPER 2 (unseen Texts and Poetry Anthology

Paper set for 1hr 30 minutes, divided into two sections.

Section A: Unseen Poetry and Prose

You answer either one question in this section : Poetry or Prose extract. Advised to use 45 minutes in this section.

Study Tips

This section test the ability to identify and comment on features of literary appreciation and includes:

– theme(s) – what the poem or extract means

Form / structure – stanza form, or absence of a stanza form Rhyme scheme, type and effect

Tone – Poetic voice : bitter, playful, ironic, etc

Diction : Language – words, repetitions and effect

Imagery : visual, Tactile, olfactory, auditory and Gustatory any other figurative devices

– Any moral lessons, effect on you etc.

Section B: Edexcel Poetry Anthology

You answer one of two questions set on poems in th Anthology. Advised to use 45 minutes in this section.

Study Tips

All the above features in section could be used in addition, Anthology section may ask question which draw comparisms between poems in the Anthology.

Edexcel IGCSE (O Level) Biology – May /June 2020 Examination Specification

The IGCSE Biology (4BIO) specification comprises two externally assessed papers:

1. Biology Paper 1

The paper is assessed through a 2 – hour examination paper set and marked by Edexcel.

The total number of marks is 120 which accounts for 66.7% of the total qualification.

2. Biology Paper 2

The paper is assessed through a 1 – hour examination paper set and marked by Edexcel. The total number of marks is 60 which accounts for 33.3% of the total qualification marks.

Content of the syllabus

The qualification content of IGCSE Biology (4BIO) is divided into five sections and students are expected to get through understanding of them. The five sections are as follows:

1. Section 1: The nature and variety of living organism

a. characteristics of living organism.

b. Variety of living organism

2. Section 2: Structures and functions in living organisms

a. Levels of organization

b. Cell structure

c. Biological molecules

d. Movement of substances into and out of cells

e. Nutrition

f. Respiration

g. Gas exchange

h. Transport

i. Excretion

j. Coordination and response

3. Section 3 : Reproduction and inheritance

a. Reproduction in flowering plants

b. Reproduction in humans

c. Inheritance

4. Section 4 : Ecology and the environment

a. The organism in the environment

b. Feeding relationship

c. Cycles within Ecosystems

d. Human influences on the environment

5. Section 5 : Uses of Biological resources

a. Food production

b. selective breeding

c. Genetic modification (genetic engineering)

d. Cloning

Note:

  • The assessment for this qualification is linear and both papers must be taken in the same series.
  • In the examination, students will be tested on the following areas:
        1. Knowledge and understanding
        2. Application of knowledge and understanding, analysis and evaluation
        3. Experiment skills, analysis and evaluation of data and methods.
